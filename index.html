The Road Less Travelled

Bangladesh's Journey from Riverways to Highways

Once upon a time, Bangladesh was a land of rivers. The mighty waterways served as the lifeblood of the nation, connecting people and places, facilitating trade, and shaping the country's destiny. But as the world changed, so did Bangladesh. The rivers, while still important, were no longer enough. The country needed a new kind of connection - roads.
